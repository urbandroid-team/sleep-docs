*Sleep tracking allows you to objectively monitor and analyze the quality of your rest.*

By tracking your sleep, the app enables you to:

* *Visualize:* View a detailed sleep graph for every night.
* *Monitor:* Track long-term trends with your <</sleep/sleepscore#, Sleep Score>> and <</sleep/statistics#,statistics>>.
* *Improve:* Set personal goals to enhance your sleep hygiene.
* *Wake Up Refreshed:* Use <</sleep/smart_wake_up#,smart wake up>> to wake up during your lightest sleep phase.

NOTE: For the science behind these features, see <</sleep/sleep_tracking_theory#, Sleep tracking theory>>.

[TIP]
Find all tracking options in _Settings -> Sleep tracking_.

== How to track sleep

To get the most accurate insights into your rest, the app utilizes one primary sensor and optionally a handful of additional sensors.

Choose from several sensor options to monitor your sleep.

[cols="1,1,1,1", options="header"]
|===
| Sensor type | Position | Pros | Cons
| *Accelerometer* | On the mattress | Easiest setup, battery efficient | X
| *Sonar* | Bedside table | Contact-less comfort, breath rate | Battery demanding
| *Wearable* | Wrist | Maximum accuracy, additional sensors | Extra device
| *SleepPhaser* | Bedside table | Smart light features, breath rate | Extra device
|===

=== Accelerometer
Every smartphone is equipped with an accelerometer. This sensor detects movement; the core principle is that as you shift during sleep, your movements vibrate the mattress, which the phone then records.

==== Positioning the phone for accelerometer tracking

For the accelerometer to work, the phone must be *placed directly on the mattress*.

.Phone placement when tracking using accelerometer
image::tracking-position/accel.png[]

The accuracy of measured data depends heavily on how well your bed is able to transmit your movement to the phone.

[color-green]#Wearable device# > [color-green]#Arm band# > [color-orange]#Spring mattress# > [color-orange]#Latex# > [color-orange]#Hard foam# > [color-orange]#Soft foam# > [color-red]#Thick slow foam layer# > [color-red]#100% Slow foam#

=== Sonar

Sleep as Android technology turns your phone’s microphone and speaker into a sonar system. By emitting ultrasonic waves, the app detects your movement and respiration through frequency shifts. This allows for *contactless tracking*.

It works on a lot of phones, but not all (some are unable to produce or capture frequencies above human hearing range).

https://sleep.urbandroid.org/introducing-sonar-as-sensor/[Read more] about sonar and how we invented it.

==== Positioning the phone for sonar tracking

.Phone placement when tracking using sonar
image::tracking-position/sonar.png[]


Because ultrasonic waves lose energy over distance, precise placement is key:

* *Surface:* Place the phone on a steady, flat surface (like a bedside table).
* *Height:* The phone should be at the same level as your body or slightly higher (chest level).
* *Direction:* Point the bottom of the phone (where the microphone usually sits) directly toward your chest.
* *Clearance:* Ensure there are no large objects (lamps, water bottles) blocking the "line of sight" between the phone and your body.

=== Other devices

If you prefer not to keep your phone near your bed at all, you can integrate external devices, like <</devices/sleep_phaser#, Sleep Phaser>>, or <</devices/wearables#, wearables>>.

== The Tracking Screen

When tracking is active, the screen provides real-time data and quick actions.

[[sleep-tracking-screen-1]]
.Tracking screen interface
image::sleep_tracking_screen_1.png[]

* *Elapsed Time:* Total duration of the current session.
* *Progress Bar:* Shows time elapsed vs. your next alarm.
* *Current Time:* Digital clock for quick reference.
* *Noise Indicator:* Shows volume levels if <</sleep/sleep_noise_recording#, Noise recording>> is on.
* *Alarm Info:* Displays your next scheduled wake-up time.
** *No alarm:* means there is no alarm for the day. Tap to add a new alarm (opens the alarm time picker)
** *Alarm time:* shows the alarm time for the day. Tap to open the per-alarm settings for quick changes.
* *Quick Actions icons:*
** icon:ic_action_pause[] *Pause:* Manually pause if you get out of bed.
** icon:ic_action_cancel[] *Stop:* End the session (requires confirmation).
** icon:ic_flashlight[] *Night ligh*: Turn on the phone's flashlight or any connected <</devices/smart_light#, Smartlight>> device.
** icon:ic_action_lullaby[] *Lullabies:* Start soothing sounds.
* *Stop and save:* Sliding up the _Stop and Save_ pull up menu will bring up further options:
** icon:ic_flashlight[] *Night ligh*: Turn on the phone's flashlight or any connected <</devices/smart_light#, Smartlight>>. It will use it at minimum brightness (and red if possible) to help you to navigate the room.
** icon:ic_pencil[] *Tags*: Add tags or notes to the current sleep entry.
** icon:ic_action_lullaby[] *Lullabies:* Start lullaby sounds.
** icon:ic_battery_60[] *Save battery*: switches sleep tracking into a low power mode. In this mode tracking will consume minimum battery, but Noise recording>will be stopped and no activity will be tracked using sensors. This is useful if you don't have much battery but still want to track the time of your sleep.
** icon:ic_action_cancel[] *Stop:* End the current sleep tracking (no confirmation needed).

[TIP]
Running sleep tracking is always indicated in the status bar as an ongoing notification. Even after leaving the tracking screen you can always get back through this notification.

== 🔍 Deep Dive

Explore advanced automation, battery optimization, and specialized features from _Settings -> Sleep tracking_.

=== Automation & Detection

* *<</sleep/automatic_sleep_tracking#, Automatic tracking>>*: Set the app to start and stop tracking based on your schedule or movement.
* *<</sleep/awake_detection#, Awake detection>>:* Smart algorithms that recognize when you are actually awake vs. just moving in your sleep.
* *<</sleep/pair_tracking#, Pair tracking>>:* Sync with a partner to filter out their movements from your data.

=== Battery & Performance

* *Battery optimized:* Enables "sensor batching." This allows the phone's CPU to sleep while the sensor collects data.
** This is unavailable when using Sonar or Noise Recording.
** Due to Doze mode, starting from Android 6 we need to schedule a user-visible alarm every minute to be able to use sensor batching. This alarm will never ring, but will be visible on your status bar and lockscreen.
** May break awake detection on some phones.
* *Stand-by:* Automatically stops tracking if your battery drops below a specific percentage to ensure your alarm still rings.

=== Comfort & Environment

* *<</sleep/lullaby#, Lullabies>>*: Play melodies, audiobooks, or white noise that automatically fades out once you fall asleep.
* *Screen Dimming:* Controls the display behavior during the night:
** *Disabled* System default timeout applies.
** *Dim* Screen darkens but stays active.
** *Dim with clock* Shows a dim clock; system timeout applies.
** *Always on clock* Keeps a dim clock visible all night. (WARNING: High battery usage).
* *Do Not Disturb (DND)*: Automatically silences notifications when you start tracking.
** *Priority Mode:* Allows emergency contacts to bypass DND while you sleep.

=== Specialized Features

* *<</sleep/lucid_dreaming#, Lucid dreaming>>*: Uses sound or light cues during REM sleep to help you realize you are dreaming.
* *Jet Lag Prevention:* Tools to help shift your circadian rhythm when traveling.
